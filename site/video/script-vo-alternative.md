# Demo video — voiceover (alternative track)

> The default version of the video has **no voiceover.** This script is
> provided only for international audiences and for platforms where
> audio-on-by-default makes silent video lose meaning (LinkedIn,
> Twitter feed). Use sparingly. The voiceover-free version is the
> primary asset.

## Tone

Calm, confident, second-person. The voice talks about the product, not
the viewer's pain. No "Tired of meetings that go nowhere?" No "Imagine
a world where..." Treat the viewer as smart and time-constrained.

The voice should sound like the founder, not a hired narrator. If a
hired narrator is necessary, find one whose default register is
documentary, not advertisement.

## Script

> **0:00–0:05** *(silent in default; ambient room tone only)*
>
> [VO option] "Instinctual lives in your menu bar."

> **0:05–0:30** *(meeting audio is the lead — VO sparse here)*

> **0:30–0:38** *(clarification beat)*

> [VO option] "When something's ambiguous, you get a one-tap question."

> **0:38–0:60** *(building)*

> **0:80–0:88** *(the timestamp card)*
>
> *(no VO — let the text breathe)*

> **0:88–0:90** *(closing)*
>
> [VO option] "Instinctual."

## Total VO word count: ~55 words

## Localization notes

- Spanish, French, German, Japanese variants needed for international
  posting in 2026 — not earlier. v0 is English only.
- For VO-translated cuts, re-record the audio against the same picture
  edit. Do not subtitle a VO version; pick one channel and commit.

## Why we default to no VO

The meeting audio is the most authentic signal we have. A narrator
talking over a real meeting reads as performance, not product. The
text-on-screen beats (timestamps, closing card) carry the verbal
content without a voice.

The VO version exists only as a backup for contexts where silent video
hurts (LinkedIn autoplay with mute on, Twitter feed). Even then, prefer
captions over voiceover when possible.
