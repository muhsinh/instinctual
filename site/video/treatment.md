# Demo video — creative treatment

## Goal

Ninety seconds. One real meeting. One working tool at the end. By the
last frame, a viewer who didn't read anything else understands what
Instinctual does and feels the cadence of it. They should be able to repeat
the pitch in one sentence after watching: *"Their meeting builds the
thing they're talking about, while they're talking about it."*

## Audience

Investors who'll spend 5 minutes with the deck and 30 minutes on a call.
Engineering leaders evaluating tools. Recruits considering joining.

The video is the asset that runs across all three. It needs to land
without context.

## Posture

Confident, ambient, technical. No talking head. No narrator. No "AI
solves problem" voiceover. The product talks — through what's on screen,
through the cadence of what builds, through the timing of when it pauses
for the human and when it doesn't.

Think Raycast's launch reels, Linear's product demos, Vercel's
deployment videos. Not a startup explainer. Not a tutorial.

## What this video must accomplish

1. **Show the menu bar icon doing something.** The icon activating, the
   panel sliding down — that motion is the pitch in five seconds. If the
   icon doesn't capture the viewer's eye, nothing else recovers.
2. **Show the spec doc forming during the meeting.** The viewer must see
   text appearing as conversation happens. Not a static screenshot, not
   "after the meeting." During.
3. **Show the clarification banner.** This is the moment the product is
   not just transcribing but thinking. A visible "I have a question" beat,
   tap to answer, dismiss. Demonstrates active intelligence.
4. **Show the meeting end → tool live transition.** The hand-off from
   "still talking" to "deployed and reachable" is the loop closing. A
   real URL in the panel, the user clicking it, the deployed tool
   running. This is the moment the pitch lands.
5. **Show the timestamp.** Meeting ended at 2:47. Tool live at 2:51.
   Four minutes. The number does work no copy can.

## What this video must NOT do

- No voiceover saying "Instinctual does X." The product shows.
- No on-screen captions narrating ("Instinctual is now listening..."). The
  product's UI handles this in-frame.
- No comparison ("Unlike Granola..."). The deck and memo do that work.
- No team / mission / story. That's the founder's letter, not a demo.
- No music with vocals. No "tech demo" library track. Bespoke ambient or
  licensed-and-distinctive only.
- No fake meeting. No two people pretending to discuss something they
  scripted. Real meeting, real consent, real outputs.
- No fast cuts. Cadence is calm. Maximum cuts: 6 in 90 seconds.

## Tone references

- **Raycast launch reels** — the entire visual language of Instinctual's
  product references theirs. Match.
- **Linear marketing videos** — minimal motion, tight type, calm cadence.
- **Vercel edge network video** — distinctive sound design without
  music, technical without being cold.

Avoid: any "AI startup explainer" video from 2024. Anything with
gradient soup. Anything narrated by a generic voiceover artist.

## Audio

Ambient room tone from the meeting itself, lightly EQ'd. No music for
the first 30 seconds. A bespoke ambient pad (~20-30s) enters at the
"Building" transition. Fades out under the closing card.

If a music budget doesn't exist, use silence with subtle UI sound
effects (panel slide, banner appearance, deploy success). Better
silence than the wrong track.

## Color and grade

Native macOS dark. No grade beyond what the screen recording produces.
The product's design system is the look — don't add LUTs, vignettes, or
"cinematic" treatment. The screen is the truth.

## Length

90 seconds, hard. Not 60, not 120. Long enough to build cadence; short
enough to embed in a deck slide and loop on a website hero.

## Shoot vs. composite

Real screen recording, real meeting, real output. No composited fake
animations. The hero `MenuBarMock` on the website is a stand-in until
this video exists; the video replaces it.
